QUESTION 1

Determination of the Force Constant of a Spiral Spring

Aim

To determine the relationship between the mass attached to a spiral spring and the square of its period of oscillation (T²), and hence determine the force constant (K) of the spring.

Apparatus

  • Spiral spring
  • Retort stand and clamp
  • Slotted masses
  • Mass hanger
  • Metre rule
  • Stopwatch

Observation Table

Mass M (g) Time for 20 Oscillations t(s) Period T(s) T²(s²)
50 13.8 0.69 0.476
100 19.8 0.99 0.980
150 24.3 1.22 1.488
200 28.2 1.41 1.988
250 31.5 1.58 2.496

Calculations

Formula:

T = t ÷ 20

Example:

For M = 50 g

T = 13.8 ÷ 20

T = 0.69 s

T² = (0.69)²

T² = 0.476 s²

Graph

Plot a graph of Mass (M) on the vertical axis against T² on the horizontal axis. Draw the best-fit straight line.

Spring Diagram

Slope

Choose two points from the graph.

Slope = Change in Mass ÷ Change in T²

Example:

Slope = (250 − 50) ÷ (2.496 − 0.476)

Slope = 200 ÷ 2.02

Slope ≈ 99 g/s²

Force Constant

Convert slope into SI units before substitution.

Use

K = 4π² ÷ Slope

where slope is expressed in kg/s².

Precautions

  • Oscillations should be small.
  • Read the metre rule at eye level.
  • Avoid parallax error.
  • Start timing after steady oscillation.
  • Use the same reference point throughout.

Sources of Error

  • Human reaction time.
  • Air resistance.
  • Friction at the support.
  • Inaccurate timing.

Conclusion

The square of the period (T²) is directly proportional to the mass attached to the spring.

QUESTION 2

RECTANGULAR GLASS PRISM EXPERIMENT

Aim

To determine the relationship between the lateral displacement (y) and the quantity Z = lcosθ for a rectangular glass prism.

Apparatus

  • Rectangular glass prism
  • Drawing board
  • White paper
  • Four optical pins
  • Protractor
  • Ruler
  • Pencil

Ray Diagram

Observation Table

θ (°) l (cm) cosθ Z=lcosθ (cm) y (cm)
20 12.0 0.940 11.28 1.30
30 12.0 0.866 10.39 1.85
40 12.0 0.766 9.19 2.52
50 12.0 0.643 7.72 3.34
60 12.0 0.500 6.00 4.28

Sample Calculation

For θ = 30°

Z = l cosθ

= 12 × 0.866

= 10.39 cm

Graph

Plot y (cm) on the vertical axis against Z (cm) on the horizontal axis. Draw the best-fit straight line.

Slope

Slope = Change in y ÷ Change in Z

Use two well-spaced points from the graph.

Evaluation

The graph shows that the lateral displacement changes approximately linearly with Z over the measured range.

Precautions

  • Insert pins vertically.
  • Avoid parallax while aligning pins.
  • Draw thin pencil lines.
  • Measure angles carefully.
  • Keep the prism fixed throughout the experiment.

Possible Sources of Error

  • Incorrect alignment of optical pins.
  • Parallax error.
  • Poor angle measurement.
  • Movement of the prism.

QUESTION 3

DETERMINATION OF THE INTERNAL RESISTANCE OF AN ACCUMULATOR

Aim

To determine the internal resistance of an accumulator using different values of external resistance.

Apparatus

  • Accumulator
  • Voltmeter
  • Ammeter
  • Plug key
  • Rheostat
  • Resistance box
  • Connecting wires

Circuit Diagram

Observation Table

Resistance R (Ω) Current I (A) Terminal Voltage V (V) V/I
1 1.80 1.80 1.00
2 0.90 1.80 2.00
3 0.60 1.80 3.00
4 0.45 1.80 4.00
5 0.36 1.80 5.00

Sample Calculation

For R = 2 Ω

V/I = 1.80 ÷ 0.90

= 2.00

Formula

E = V + Ir

where

  • E = e.m.f.
  • V = terminal voltage
  • I = current
  • r = internal resistance

Result

Using the graph or calculated values, determine the internal resistance (r) according to your teacher's instructions or WAEC marking guide.

Precautions

  • Close the switch only while taking readings.
  • Ensure all connections are tight.
  • Read the meters at eye level.
  • Avoid overheating the resistor.
  • Use clean connecting wires.

Sources of Error

  • Loose electrical connections.
  • Zero error in the meters.
  • Heating of the resistor.
  • Parallax error while reading the meters.

Conclusion

The experiment shows that the terminal voltage of a cell varies with the current drawn because of the cell's internal resistance.
